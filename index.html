2) Problem Description and Motivation

Knowing when to buy flight tickets is tricky as they fluctuate seemingly
unpredictably. Our objective is to produce a model that can predict how
far in advance one should purchase a flight ticket.

3) Methods

  a) Preprocessing methods
    i)  Visualization
      For each flight, a data point was created containing a column of ticket prices
      and a column containing the number of days. This matrix was used to create a
      scatterplot of prices over different number of days befor the plane's departure
   ii)   Removing Outliers
      After visualizing the data, some of the scatterplots showed signs of outliers.
      We created a replica of the data with outliers removed. This creates gaps in the data,
      but keeps the average flight price from becoming skewed, especially by high prices.
      This is important for accurately finding less than average prices
  iii)   Normalization
      The range of flight prices vary depending on the flight. To compare the patterns
      of flight prices between different flights, the flight prices were normalized.
      Because of normalization, expensive flight prices are known to be close to 1,
      and inexpensive flight prices are known to be close to 0 regardless of flight.

Flight Price with Respect to Days Before Departure




  b) Supervised Learning methods

      Random Tree Regression was chosen as the first machine learning model
      because it showed the best results according to previous research into
      flight price prediction. It was also reccommended as being a good introductory
      supervised learning method.
