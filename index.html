1) Introduction

  a) Literature Review
    i) Lu was concerned with flight prices as a time-series and
     when to buy/wait. Lu found the AdaBoost-DecisionTree
     algorithm had the best performance (61.35% than random
     purchase strategy) over the eight routes examined; K-means
     and EM to address outliers.
   ii)  S. Gupta and N. Gupta used a combination of decision tree
     algorithms, k Nearest Neighbors (KNN), and linear
     regression on historical trends to forecast flight prices.
     Their Random Forest Regressor and Decision Tree Regressor
     had r2 scores of .81 and .67 respectively.
  iii)  Tziridis et al. evaluated different regression models
     including random forests and support vector machines on a
     dataset of over 1,000 flights, focusing on how departure time
     and the number of stops a given flight had would impact the
     prediction accuracy. They found that the bagging regression
     tree model had the highest accuracy of 87.42%.
  b) Dataset Description
    i) The data will contain prices of flights over time, consisting
     of fixed destinations and dates. For example, we will only
     consider specific flights from ATL to LAX, NY, and CUN
     (popular destinations) and focus on dates when flights are
     popular (Thanksgiving, Christmas, etc.).
  c) Dataset Link: Data will be scraped from Google Flights.

2) Problem Description and Motivation

Knowing when to buy flight tickets is tricky as they fluctuate seemingly
unpredictably. Our objective is to produce a model that can predict how
far in advance one should purchase a flight ticket.

3) Methods

  a) Preprocessing methods
    i) One Hot Encoding can/will be a way for us to turn certain
     features into numerical data. Some features that could
     benefit from this are the Airline name, and departure and
     arrival locations.
   ii)  Standard Scaling can be used to normalize the data. Standard
     scaling makes sure features are transformed such that they
     have a mean of 0 and a standard deviation of 1. This prevents
     any single feature from over influencing the model
  iii)  Polynomial Features is also beneficial to flight price
     prediction as there might not be a linear relationship
     between features and the flight prices. Polynomial features
     allows us to make the label dependent on several features
     like flight duration, arrival destination, and the flight date.
  b) ML Algorithms/Models
    i)  K Means Clustering
    ii)  Hierarchical Clustering
   iii)  GMM
   iv)  Hidden Markov Models
    v)  Singular Value Decomposition
   vi)  General Adversarial Networks
  vii)  Principle Component Analysis
  c) Supervised Learning methods
    i)  Random Forest Regressor
    ii)  Decision Tree Regressor
   iii)  Support Vector Regressor
   iv)  Decision Tree
    v)  AdaBoost
   vi)  Bagging Regression Trees

4) Results and Discussion

  a) Quantitative Metrics
    1. Price of plane tickets
    2. Days before departure
    3. Additional costs such as checked baggage
  b) Project Goals
    i) Identify how many days before departure one should book
     flights from Atlanta based on destination, date of travel, and
     personal customer preferences like (baggage, airline, etc.) in
     order to minimize the price of the plane ticket.
   ii)  Determine during what seasons are prices of plane tickets
     the cheapest.
  c) Expected Results
   We expect that prices of plane tickets are most expensive a month
   before the date of departure. Prices should be cheapest several
   months before the date of departure, but not immediately after the
   flight's posting.
